convenience

Our prior research suggested that nostalgia and escapism were powerful motivators in seeking content. Even in one-on-one interviews or in participants that claimed they generally watched TV on their own, social experiences that were particularly memorable would come up organically and fondly. It brought a sense of comfort and connection in a medium that we can be passive observers of.

We envisioned a future of convenience through automated content editing and content creation, motivated by the following insights:

01
People want to use content capturing to escape from current reality by reflecting or re-experiencing their past
02
People want to “mold” the content they capture to present their stories for certain audiences

The possibilities

01
The Power of Nostalgia

One of the most commonly stated uses of future technology was to reconnect with the past. Synthetic content creation enables us to experience memories as close as we can to the root with more detailed and realistic ways to engage senses despite physical barriers.

02
Escapism

The fidelity of new immersive technologies means experiencing the risky situations without risk and escaping to unattainable places. We have always used TV for escapism, to get lost in stories of other people and watch new worlds unfold. In the future, we can harness that power individually to create our own scenarios and circumstances.

03
Co-creating Content & Shared Experiences

The content creation process is much more enjoyable and less laborious when it’s done with other people. People are essential in creating warm, nostalgic memories as well. Participants brought up who they shared their memories with rather than specific physical artifacts from their time together.

04
Becoming Content Creators

Giving us individual power over tailoring content for small groups removes the fears we may have of sharing content with a wide audience that we can’t gauge the reaction of. Having content flow from our mind to synthetically come to life in front of us could remove the learning curve of film technology and make creation more accessible to the average user.

personalization

Despite existing access to intelligent agents, research has indicated that people still experience friction when trying to achieve their desired content creation or viewing experience through these assistive systems. This opens a new door of opportunities where virtual characters can better adapt to users’ content needs and extend their content experiences by consuming it from multiple perspectives.

We envisioned a future of personalization where intelligent agents and digital characters would enable people to bridge the realms of reality and imagination, as motivated by the following insights:

01
People want the TV experience to adapt to their existing lifestyle
02
People want an intelligent system to anticipate their needs
03
People want to experience content from a different perspective

The possibilities

01
Synthetic Creatures and Collectibles

While many participants were concerned with the idea of synthetically generating or recreating real-life people, it was not the case with other entities such as inanimate object, animals or fictional characters. This leads to a possible opportunity to recreate real-life relationships users have with objects and creatures.

02
Intellegent Virtual Assistant

A surprising modality to creating a feeling of companionship with a digital entity along with a prolonged relationship is through adaptive and intelligent systems. The prefix of “smart”-anything usually constitutes an enhanced utilitarian function that is made to streamline experiences. Intelligent systems that understand the user on a deeper, more personal level creates a more “human” interaction.

03
Reinforced Learning

With a great emphasis on building empathy through realism, group discussions also speculated around leveraging this nurturing of empathy to reinforce learning endeavors. One participant in particular made the comparison between traditional textual based learning with “getting to live it”, and how it's a “better way to learn for many people”.

socialization

Whether they are cooking, working, or hanging out in the living room, TV viewers have expressed the need to adjust the way they view and digest digital content based on the context in which they interact with it. Particularly in social contexts, where multiple users have different preferences and needs, a TV that can adapt and mediate the needs of multiple parties can enhance the collective viewing experience.

We envisioned a future of socialization through adaptive digital content, motivated by the following insights:

01
People want to use content to indulge in their emotions
02
People choose to watch TV because they can easily switch their attention to and from other tasks
03
People want to have collective experiences when watching with other people

The possibilities

01
Collaborative Digital Experiences

When in a social environment, technology can help mediate or identify the collective preferences and needs of the group. In addition, synthetic content can be used to dynamically modify the content based on individual needs, thereby maximizing individual enjoyment of the shared content.

02
Adapting Content to Attention

Through synthetic content, depending on the cognitive load that the user has allocated for other tasks, the TV content should dynamically adjust the information density and the channels by which content is presented. For instance, when performing a visually-demanding task, users could instead listen to TV content.

03
Multimodal Channels of Output

Synthetic content can be used to alter the channels by which users experience content as they attend to other tasks. Beyond just visual and audio channels, we envision a future where content can be consumed through haptic feedback, which can be adjusted dynamically based on the user’s context and needs.

04
Mirroring Emotions with Content

Content creators and viewers alike frequently tailor their content experiences according to their present mood and emotions. By detecting these mood shifts, synthetic content can be used not only to better tailor digital content for viewers, but also to better filter and mold content for creators as they capture moments in their lives.
